# Karaka Pines Villages — Brand Guidelines

**Source:** www.karakapines.co.nz and primary logo assets in `reference/`
**Drafted:** April 2026
**Status:** Working draft — derived from public website and supplied logo files. Confirm exact Pantone/CMYK values with KPV marketing before print production.

---

## 1. Brand essence

**Tagline:** *You Have So Much More To Gain*
**Supporting line:** *Better living in every way*

Karaka Pines Villages positions itself as the fair, resident-first alternative in the New Zealand retirement village sector. The brand leans on three ideas:

1. **Fairness** — residents benefit from capital gain rather than forfeiting it.
2. **Freedom and choice** — lifestyle, not dependency.
3. **Community and warmth** — villages feel like homes, not institutions.

**Personality:** warm, dignified, trustworthy, optimistic, quietly confident. Never clinical, corporate-cold, or hard-sell.

## 2. Logo

### Primary mark
A navy circular roundel containing:
- A stylised white **"K" monogram** with a flowing koru-like flourish down the left stroke
- A **green leaf** emerging from the top-right of the K
- The wordmark **"Karaka Pines"** in a classical serif, white
- **"VILLAGES"** in spaced, light-grey sans-serif caps beneath

The roundel, monogram, leaf, and wordmark are a single lockup. Do not redraw, recolour the individual elements, or separate the leaf from the K.

### Clear space
Maintain clear space around the roundel equal to at least the height of the word **"VILLAGES"** on all sides. Nothing (text, imagery, edges) should enter this zone.

### Minimum size
- Digital: 48 px diameter minimum
- Print: 15 mm diameter minimum

Below this size the serif wordmark and "VILLAGES" caps become illegible.

### Acceptable variations
- **Full colour on white** (preferred)
- **Full colour on light photography** with sufficient contrast
- **Reversed / white on navy** for dark backgrounds
- **Single-colour navy** where colour reproduction is not possible

### Misuse — do not
- Stretch, skew, or rotate the roundel
- Change the navy or swap the leaf colour
- Place the mark on low-contrast or busy photographic backgrounds
- Add drop shadows, outlines, glows, or effects
- Recreate the wordmark in a different typeface
- Crop the roundel or use the K monogram on its own as a standalone mark without written approval

**Rules of thumb**
- Navy and white do the heavy lifting. Green is an accent — never a background colour for large blocks.
- Use Action Green for a single primary call-to-action per view. Avoid multiple competing green buttons.
- Never place the leaf green next to the action green — they clash.
- Avoid bright reds, oranges, or purples. They are not part of the brand.

## 4. Typography

The website uses a system sans-serif stack for performance; the logo uses a classical serif. Brand materials should echo this pairing.

### Print and brand collateral
- **Headings:** a classical serif (the logo uses a Trajan / Cinzel-style capital set; for body headings a readable book serif such as **Adobe Caslon**, **Garamond**, or **Source Serif** works well).
- **Body:** a humanist sans-serif such as **Source Sans**, **Open Sans**, or **Lato**.

### Digital
- **Font stack:** `-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", Roboto, sans-serif`
- **H1:** ~2.67 rem, bold, 1.3 line-height
- **H2:** ~2.13 rem, bold
- **H3:** ~1.73 rem, bold
- **Body:** 1 rem (~15 px), 1.65 line-height
- **Subtitle caps:** letter-spaced, uppercase, sparingly (echoes the "VILLAGES" treatment)

### Rules
- Never set "Karaka Pines" in a sans-serif to create a fake wordmark. Use the logo file.
- Keep body copy at comfortable reading sizes — the audience is older adults and their families. Avoid anything below 14 px on screen or 10 pt in print.
- Generous line-height and paragraph spacing — the brand should feel unhurried.

## 5. Imagery and photography

### Subjects
- Real residents in genuine moments — conversation, gardening, walking, shared meals.
- Village architecture, gardens, landscaped grounds.
- Wide exterior shots that show New Zealand light and landscape.

### Style
- **Natural, warm light** — prefer golden-hour, soft morning, or bright overcast. Avoid harsh flash or clinical fluorescent.
- **Candid over posed.** Genuine expressions, not stock-photo smiles to camera.
- **Spacious composition.** Breathing room around subjects.
- **Authentic New Zealand context** — native plants, local architecture, recognisable light.

### Avoid
- Staged stock photography with generic older-person-on-bench clichés
- Heavily filtered or colour-graded images (especially cold blue tones or vintage sepia)
- Medical, clinical, or dependency-framed imagery (walking frames, clinical settings) unless genuinely part of the story
- Tight, cluttered compositions

### Botanical motif
The logo leaf reflects a wider botanical sensibility. Koru, karaka leaves, and pine elements can appear subtly — watermarks, section dividers, background patterns — but should not overwhelm layouts.

## 6. Tone of voice

### Voice principles
1. **Warm but grown-up.** Speak to residents as capable adults making an informed decision, not as patients or problems to be solved.
2. **Plain NZ English.** Short sentences. Everyday words. NZ spelling (*colour, organise, realise, favourite, centre*).
3. **Specific, not aspirational waffle.** "You keep 100% of the capital gain on your home" beats "unlock your future".
4. **Fair and factual.** The fairness story is the strongest differentiator — lead with it plainly, not with hype.
5. **Quietly proud.** Confident without being boastful.

### Do
- "You have so much more to gain."
- "A fair deal that protects what you've built."
- "More freedom, more choice, more of what matters."

### Don't
- "Revolutionary retirement solutions" — corporate-speak.
- "Seniors" — prefer "residents" or describe people specifically.
- "Downsizing" as a headline — the brand is about gaining, not giving up.
- Exclamation marks. Ever, really.
- Fabricated statistics or unverifiable claims.

### Addressing the audience
Speak directly — *you*, *your*. The primary reader is the prospective resident; the secondary reader is an adult child researching on a parent's behalf. Both should feel respected.

## 7. Layout and design patterns

- **Generous whitespace.** The brand feels unhurried — crowded layouts undermine it.
- **Cards:** white background, soft shadow, rounded corners no larger than 4–8 px. Avoid heavy borders.
- **Buttons:** filled Action Green with white text for primary; outlined navy for secondary. Rounded corners should match cards.
- **Dividers:** thin rules in `#E5E5E5`, or generous whitespace instead.
- **Icons:** simple, line-based, consistent stroke weight. Avoid flat-colour illustrations that compete with photography.
- **Hero sections:** full-bleed photography with a single clear headline and one call-to-action. Never two competing CTAs in a hero.

---

## 8. Naming and nomenclature

- The brand is **Karaka Pines Villages** (plural). Use in full on first reference.
- Subsequent references: **Karaka Pines** or **KPV** (internal/written, not marketing).
- Individual villages are named by location — refer to the sheet registry and website for the authoritative list of the eight locations.
- "Village" (singular) refers to a single location; "Villages" (plural) refers to the group or brand.
