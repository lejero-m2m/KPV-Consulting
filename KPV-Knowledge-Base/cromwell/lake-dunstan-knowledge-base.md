## 1. Project Overview

Lake Dunstan is a proposed 100-unit retirement village development on a 4.91 ha site in the Cromwell area of Central Otago. The project is at feasibility stage. The land is structured as an operating lease (not freehold purchase). The feasibility model runs over a 15-year term commencing 30/06/2026.

The development is a joint venture between Hapai (majority A-class shareholder) and KPV (B-class shareholder). No vendor equity is allocated in the current model.

### 7.2 HUE Assessment for Retirement Villages

**Policy paragraph 43** provides a specific HUE rate for retirement villages:

> "A retirement unit or visitor accommodation unit will be assessed as generating **0.5 HUEs** for each activity."

This is a standalone classification based on the nature of the development. It does not cross-reference the minor/small/standard bedroom-based assessment in Table 5 (paragraphs 44-45), which applies to ordinary residential units.

**Correct calculation at 0.5 HUE (policy paragraph 43):**
- 100 units x 0.5 HUE = 50 HUEs
- 50 x $34,225 = **$1,711,250** (GST inclusive)

### 7.3 What the Feasibility Model Uses

The model workings sidebar (cells L136:O145, Project Inputs sheet) correctly lists per-HUE charges that match the CODC policy Table 1 exactly. However, cell H141 is **hardcoded at $2,700,000** -- this does not correspond to any standard HUE calculation:

| Scenario | HUEs | Total DC |
|---|---|---|
| 0.5 HUE flat (policy para 43) | 50.0 | $1,711,250 |
| Hardcoded in model (H141) | ~78.9 implied | $2,700,000 |
| 1.0 HUE standard residential | 100.0 | $3,422,500 |

### 7.4 Colleague's Blended HUE Proposal

A colleague proposed applying CODC Table 5 (minor/small/standard) bedroom-based rates to retirement village units:

| Unit type | Qty | Proposed HUE | Total HUEs |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-bed (R6) | 20 | 0.50 | 10.0 |
| 2-bed (BSG) | 50 | 0.75 | 37.5 |
| 3-bed (MNSG) | 30 | 1.00 | 30.0 |
| **Total** | **100** | | **77.5** |

77.5 x $34,225 = **$2,652,438**

**This approach conflates two separate assessment mechanisms.** Table 5 applies to minor/small residential units in ordinary subdivisions, not to retirement villages. Paragraph 43 is the specific provision for retirement villages and sets a flat 0.5 HUE regardless of bedroom count. A 3-bed duplex in a retirement village is still a retirement village unit.

### 7.5 Recommendation

Model the DCs at **0.5 HUE flat** ($1,711,250). Flag the risk that Council may seek a higher assessment under its discretionary power in paragraph 43 ("Council will determine the most appropriate classification based on the nature of the development"). Do not concede a higher rate in the feasibility model without first confirming CODC's position.

### 7.6 Financial Contributions (District Plan)

The CODC policy also provides for **financial contributions for reserves** under the District Plan (Table 9):
- Reserve -- Urban: $2,900 per allotment or dwelling (GST incl)
- Reserve -- Rural: $1,450 per allotment or dwelling (GST incl)

Per policy paragraph 88, financial contributions will only be collected for reserves if a development contribution cannot be levied. Since Cromwell is within the development contribution catchment for reserves, the financial contribution likely does not stack on top. **Confirm with CODC.**

### 7.7 Service Connection Fees (separate from DCs)

Per the Fees and Charges Schedule 2025/26, in addition to development contributions, three waters new connections attract:

- Wastewater: connected per month -- $45.60 (already rated as serviceable) or $91.20 (not previously rated)
- Water supply: connected per month -- $28.53 (already rated) or $57.06 (not previously rated)

## 14. Cromwell Context (Spatial Framework)

Source: *Cromwell 'Eye to the Future' Masterplan -- Spatial Framework, Stage 1: Spatial Plan Report* (adopted 29/05/2019). Prepared for CODC by N M Associates Ltd + Tract Consultants Pty Ltd.

### 14.1 Growth Projections

The Spatial Framework plans for Cromwell to grow from approximately 5,000 people to approximately 12,000 by 2050 ("Cromwell 2050"). The population is expected to roughly double within the 30-year planning period.

### 14.2 Vision

> "Embracing opportunities that protect, share and enhance our 'World of Difference' values now and in the future"

Key desired outcomes:
- An attractive, vibrant, and thriving heart for Cromwell
- Growth accommodated while protecting landscapes and visual amenity
- Housing is affordable and available to meet demand
- A thriving and competitive local economy

### 14.3 Key Principles

1. Protect and celebrate the valued landscape, conservation, and heritage setting
2. Celebrate the horticultural, viticultural, and agricultural environment
3. Foster increased diversity in housing choices
4. Reinforce an authentic local character and identity
5. Support a healthy, diverse, and welcoming community
6. Create a compact, walking, cycling, and accessible town

### 14.4 Spatial Plan Key Moves

The Spatial Framework identifies growth areas, nodes, and connectivity improvements across the Cromwell basin including Bannockburn, Lowburn, and Pisa Moorings. The plan would be implemented primarily through Council-led Plan Changes and/or District Plan Review.

### 14.5 Existing Retirement Housing in Cromwell

The Spatial Framework notes (p.14) that *"an area of retirement housing"* exists in the 'Big Box' area adjacent to the town centre, east of SH6. The Lake Dunstan area is described as *"a significant landscape and body of water providing an altered context to Cromwell's environmental setting"* with *"'waterfront' settings, views and recreational areas for camping, picnicking, boating, fishing and swimming."*

### 14.6 Historical Context

- Cromwell was initially a gold mining settlement, incorporated as a borough in 1866
- The Clyde Dam development (1980s) flooded parts of old Cromwell, creating Lake Dunstan
- Population was relatively static at ~1,000 from the post-gold rush era through to the early 1970s
- Hydro development phase brought significant growth and reconfiguration
- Current morphology includes 'East Cromwell' (grid pattern, ~150 ha, ~8.5 dwellings/ha) and 'West Cromwell' (newer, cul-de-sac pattern, ~4 dwellings/ha)

## 16. Issues and Actions

### 16.1 Development Contributions -- Reconcile Model

**Issue:** Cell H141 in the feasibility model is hardcoded at $2,700,000. This does not match either the 0.5 HUE retirement village rate ($1,711,250) or the 1.0 HUE standard rate ($3,422,500).

**Action:** Confirm HUE assessment with CODC. Replace the hardcoded figure with a formula referencing the workings (e.g. `=O145 * HUE_factor * unit_count`). If 0.5 HUE is accepted, the model overstates DCs by ~$989,000.

### 16.2 Financial Contributions -- Confirm Non-Stacking

**Issue:** Both development contributions (reserve land, reserve improvements) and financial contributions ($2,900/dwelling urban) exist for reserves. Policy para 88 says financial contributions only apply if development contributions cannot be levied.

**Action:** Confirm with CODC that the $2,900 financial contribution does not apply on top of the DC reserve charges.

### 16.3 Stormwater -- No DC but Costs Exist

**Issue:** CODC levies no development contribution for stormwater in Cromwell, but the model includes $701,500 for stormwater infrastructure as a land development cost. This is developer-funded.

**Action:** No action required -- the cost is captured in the land development section. Confirm there are no additional CODC requirements for stormwater management (e.g. attenuation, quality treatment) that are not yet costed.

### 16.4 RMA Status

**Issue:** The project files include a 2008 RMA reprint which is out of date. The Resource Management Act 1991 has been subject to significant amendment and potential replacement by successor legislation.

**Action:** Confirm the current status of the RMA and any successor legislation (Natural and Built Environment Act / Spatial Planning Act status) against legislation.govt.nz before relying on RMA provisions.

### 16.5 Code of Practice Version

**Issue:** Two versions of the Retirement Villages Code of Practice are referenced in the project files -- 2008 (with 2017 variations) and November 2018 (v4.5). It is unclear which is currently in force.

**Action:** Verify the operative version against Te Ara Ahunga Ora (Retirement Commissioner) website.

### 16.6 Resource Consent Budget

**Issue:** The model allocates $500,000 for resource consent. For a 100-unit retirement village in Central Otago, this is a significant sum but may be appropriate if a notified consent or hearing is anticipated.

**Action:** Confirm consenting pathway with planner. If non-notified, the consent cost may be lower. If the Fast Track Act 2024 pathway is considered, costs are charged at cost on a time basis per the Fees and Charges Schedule.

### 16.7 Missing Project Inputs

The following have not been provided and would be needed to progress past feasibility:

- Site address / legal description / title reference
- Geotechnical report
- LIM / PIM
- Concept plans / site layout
- Yield study
- Market demand report for Central Otago retirement housing
- Planner's and architect's preliminary advice
- Traffic impact assessment
- Three waters capacity assessment from CODC
- Natural hazards assessment (flooding, seismic, liquefaction)
