Document Purpose
This document presents a market demand analysis for a proposed retirement village on the shores of Lake Dunstan, Cromwell, Central Otago. It examines demographic drivers, population growth, competitive supply, and absorption capacity to support an investment decision.
1.  Executive Summary
Cromwell and the wider Lake Dunstan catchment represent one of New Zealand’s most compelling underdeveloped retirement village markets. The town is the fastest-growing in Central Otago, benefits from exceptional lifestyle amenity, and is within 60 kilometres of Queenstown and Wanaka — two of the country’s highest-income retiree migration destinations. Yet retirement village supply remains critically thin relative to the size and trajectory of the 75+ population.
Key findings from this study:
Cromwell’s population has grown by over 70% since 2006, continuing at approximately 9.7% per annum — among the fastest in New Zealand.
The Central Otago District 65+ population is projected to grow materially through 2048, driven by natural ageing and active lifestyle in-migration.
The Otago region’s retirement village penetration rate has declined below the national average of 14% for the 75+ cohort — signalling supply shortage, not saturation.
Golden View Village, the only purpose-built retirement village in Cromwell, filled rapidly upon opening — independently corroborating latent demand in the market.
A lakefront or lake-view site would command premium positioning nationally, attracting both local retirees and out-of-region movers from higher-cost centres.
National JLL research (2025) identifies a shortfall of over 23,000 units nationally by 2048, with shortfall concentrated in growth and lifestyle regions such as Central Otago.
2.  Study Area & Catchment Definition
2.1  Primary Catchment: Cromwell Township
The primary catchment is the Cromwell urban area and the immediate Lake Dunstan environs, including Bannockburn, Lowburn, and Ripponvale. This zone captures the population most likely to seek retirement living within a familiar community, and those drawn specifically by the Lake Dunstan lifestyle asset.
Cromwell is the largest town in the Central Otago District, with a 2023 Census population of 7,470 and an estimated resident population approaching 8,000 by mid-2025. The town sits at the junction of State Highways 6 and 8B, providing connectivity to Queenstown (60 km), Wanaka (50 km), and Alexandra (33 km).
2.2  Secondary Catchment: Central Otago District
The secondary catchment encompasses the broader Central Otago District (population 24,306 at 2023 Census). Residents of Alexandra, Clyde, and surrounding townships are realistic candidates for a premium lakefront retirement setting offering services unavailable locally. The Aurum on Clutha project in Clyde will serve the Alexandra/Clyde sub-market independently, effectively directing Cromwell-focused demand toward the Lake Dunstan corridor.
2.3  Tertiary (Retiree Migration) Catchment
A distinctive feature of the Cromwell market is its attractiveness as a retiree migration destination from higher-cost urban centres — particularly Queenstown (where retirement affordability is severely constrained), Dunedin, and to a lesser extent Christchurch and Auckland. Lake Dunstan’s profile as a water-based lifestyle precinct, combined with Central Otago wine country and mountain biking infrastructure (the Lake Dunstan Trail attracted 62,500 riders in its first 10 months alone), makes it credible as a premium inter-regional draw.

3.1  Cromwell Population Growth

This growth has been driven by lifestyle in-migration from Queenstown and Wanaka, expansion of the horticultural and viticulture sectors, and the region’s growing profile as an outdoor recreation destination. The Central Otago District Council’s 30-Year Cromwell Masterplan explicitly plans for continued rapid growth.
3.2  Ageing Population Trajectory
New Zealand’s 65+ population reached 828,600 in 2023 (16.6% of total population), up from 715,200 in 2018. Statistics NZ projects this cohort will reach approximately 1 million by 2028 and grow to 1.9 million (28.2% of population) by 2073.
For retirement village operators, the critical cohort is 75+. Nationally, 383,510 New Zealanders were aged 75+ in 2023, with this figure projected to more than double to 759,630 by 2043 — a 98% increase in 20 years.
Source: Stats NZ National Population Projections 2024(base)–2078; JLL NZ Retirement Villages Research 2025.
3.3  Central Otago Ageing Profile
Central Otago has historically had a relatively balanced age structure, but its rapid in-migration of 30–64 year-olds over the past decade means a significant wave of this cohort will transition into the 65–75+ demographic over 2025–2040. The region’s median income broadly aligns with the national median ($41,800 in 2023), while property values are substantially elevated in Cromwell (average $745,000+), suggesting asset-rich retirees likely to qualify for retirement village ORAs.
Lifestyle-driven in-migration from urban centres further skews the ageing demographic toward higher-net-worth individuals with active retirement expectations — precisely the profile best suited to premium retirement village living.
4.  National Retirement Village Sector Context
4.1  Supply & Demand Dynamics
The New Zealand retirement village sector has grown from 343 villages in 2012 to 491 villages with 43,598 total units by year-end 2024 (JLL). Despite this, sector growth has consistently lagged demographic demand. JLL’s 2025 Annual Review identifies a potential shortfall of more than 23,000 units by 2048, requiring an additional 932 units per annum above current build rates to close the gap.
4.2  Penetration Rates by Region
Regional penetration rates for the 75+ cohort vary significantly. The Otago region has been among the regions experiencing declining penetration rates, meaning supply growth has not kept pace with population ageing.
Source: JLL NZ Retirement Villages & Aged Care Report 2024; RNZ 2024.
4.3  Lifestyle Village Premium Positioning
A demonstrable shift is underway toward retirement villages offering strong lifestyle amenity — water access, wine country, cycling trails, mountain views — that align with the active ‘young-old’ (65–80) cohort who are often the first movers into village living. This cohort delays the transition to care-integrated villages but exhibits high willingness-to-pay for premium independent living, particularly in scenic locations.
Lake Dunstan offers a lakefront lifestyle proposition with few equivalents nationally. Comparable scenic-location villages in Taupo, Queenstown, or Nelson consistently command ORA price premiums of 15–30% above the regional average.
5.  Competitive Supply Analysis
5.1  Cromwell / Lake Dunstan Direct Supply
Current retirement village supply within the Cromwell catchment is limited to a single registered operator:
Golden View Lifestyle Village comprises single, two, and three-bedroom villas set around a central lodge with landscaped grounds. Notably, the Cromwell mayor publicly cited the speed with which Golden View filled as evidence of unmet demand in Central Otago — a powerful independent validation of market depth.
5.2  Wider Central Otago Pipeline Supply
The Aurum on Clutha village in Clyde (resource consent granted 2021) is the only significant pipeline development in the district. Designed for a 7.8 ha site near Muttontown Rd, Clyde, it is planned to serve the Alexandra/Clyde sub-market. No new village development has been announced specifically for Cromwell or the Lake Dunstan corridor.
5.3  Competitive Landscape Summary
5.4  Queenstown as a Retiree Migration Driver
Queenstown Lakes District — 60 km to the west — is among New Zealand’s most expensive residential markets with a median house price above $1.2 million. Many equity-rich retirees seek to exit the Queenstown market to crystallise capital and fund retirement village occupation rights agreements. Cromwell, positioned as an affordable, scenic alternative with direct access to the same outdoor lifestyle, captures meaningful demand from this out-migration flow.
6.  Demand Modelling
6.1  Methodology
Demand is modelled using a population-based penetration rate approach, consistent with JLL methodology and industry standard practice for New Zealand retirement village feasibility analysis. The model proceeds from:
Estimated 75+ population in the catchment area (primary + secondary)
Applied penetration rate scenarios (conservative / base / optimistic)
Unit demand derivation using 1.3 residents per unit occupancy ratio
Subtracted existing supply (94 units, Golden View)
Output: net additional units required by study horizon (2030 / 2035)
6.2  Catchment 75+ Population Estimates
Cromwell’s 2023 population was 7,470. Applying the national proportion of 75+ (approximately 7.7% of total population), the Cromwell primary catchment has approximately 575 residents aged 75+. The broader Central Otago District (population 24,306) contributes an estimated 1,870 people aged 75+. Together the combined catchment 75+ population is approximately 2,450.
Note: Pipeline supply assumes Aurum on Clutha (Clyde) serves Alexandra/Clyde sub-market and does not materially compete with a premium Lake Dunstan product. Catchment 75+ population grown at 3.5% p.a.
6.3  Migration Premium Adjustment
The above model is based solely on resident population and standard penetration rates. Lake Dunstan has an additional demand driver: retiree in-migration from Queenstown, Wanaka, Dunedin, and urban centres. Applying a conservative 10–15% upward adjustment for this in-migration demand adds a further 20–35 units to the base case, bringing adjusted net demand to approximately 200–300 units by 2035.
6.4  Sensitivity to Penetration Rate Assumptions
Even at the most conservative penetration rate assumption (10%), the model still indicates unmet demand for over 100 additional units — confirming that any realistic scenario supports new village development.
7.  Site & Location Assessment
7.1  Lake Dunstan as a Development Location
Lake Dunstan was formed by the Clyde Dam in the early 1990s and has since become the defining amenity of the Cromwell basin. It offers 26 km of navigable freshwater, surrounded by schist rock ridges, vineyard terraces, and mountain panoramas. Key location attributes relevant to a retirement village development:
Year-round mild, sunny climate — Cromwell averages approximately 1,900+ sunshine hours per annum, among the highest for an inland South Island location
Water-based recreation: kayaking, sailing, fishing, lake walks — all amenable to the active 65–75 demographic
The 55 km Lake Dunstan Trail (Cromwell to Clyde), a world-class cycle and walking trail, provides direct recreational infrastructure
Wine country: Bannockburn, Lowburn, and Pisa Range vineyards provide a premium hospitality overlay
Low crime rate (0.013 vs 4.86 national average) — a key decision criterion for retirees
7.2  Site Selection Considerations
An optimal Lake Dunstan retirement village site would have the following characteristics:
Lakefront or first-row lake-view position, with direct or permissioned access to the foreshore or trail network
Proximity to Cromwell town centre (within 2–3 km) for amenity access (supermarket, medical, pharmacy, library)
Site area of 3–7 ha to support a 100–200 unit village with communal facilities
Zoning: residential or rural-residential with pathway to retirement village consent under the Central Otago District Plan
North to northwest-facing aspect for solar exposure and views to Lake Dunstan and the Pisa Range

8.4  Care Integration
While the primary product is independent living, the medium-term continuum of care should be addressed. A ‘care-ready’ design standard (wider doorways, reinforced bathrooms, hardwired emergency call) enables the village to transition residents to in-home support services as needs increase. A memorandum of understanding with Dunstan Hospital (located in Clyde) should be explored to formalise a referral and care pathway for residents requiring hospital-level care.
9.  Absorption & Timing
9.1  Absorption Rate Assumptions
Sales absorption for a well-positioned village in a constrained market (limited competition, demonstrated demand, premium lifestyle) is typically modelled at 15–30 units per annum at this price point. Golden View Village’s rapid fill-up suggests the Cromwell market can support the upper end of this range.
Stage 1 development (50–60 units) followed by staged release of subsequent tranches is the recommended delivery strategy, limiting initial capital exposure while demonstrating market velocity.
9.2  Timing Considerations
The 2026–2030 window is likely the optimal development entry point for the Lake Dunstan market:
The 75+ cohort peak growth nationally occurs around 2028 — the demand wave is beginning now
There is currently no announced competing village within the Cromwell/Lake Dunstan sub-market
Construction costs have shown signs of stabilisation in Q1 2026
Central Otago District Council has a growth-supportive planning framework under the Cromwell Masterplan
10.  Risk Assessment
11.  Conclusions & Recommendations
11.1  Demand Conclusion
The evidence strongly supports the existence of material unmet demand for retirement village accommodation in the Lake Dunstan / Cromwell catchment. The combination of:
Cromwell’s exceptional population growth trajectory (~9.7% p.a.)
A rapidly ageing national and regional demographic profile
Critically thin competitive supply (94 units against a catchment of 8,000+ and growing)
Demonstrated market validation via Golden View’s rapid fill-up
Premium lifestyle amenity (lake, wine country, cycling, sunshine, safety)
Retiree migration pressure from overpriced adjacent markets (Queenstown/Wanaka)
...creates a structural demand opportunity for 150–300 additional retirement village units by 2035, with the base case demand estimate sitting at approximately 200 units.

Appendix B: Key Definitions
ORA (Occupation Right Agreement): The legal instrument under the Retirement Villages Act 2003 by which residents acquire the right to occupy a unit in a retirement village.
DMF (Deferred Management Fee): A fee payable to the village operator upon a resident’s departure, calculated as a percentage of the ORA entry price per year of occupation (commonly 10–30% of ORA price).
Penetration Rate: The proportion of the 75+ age cohort living in registered retirement villages. The national average is approximately 14%.
Continuum of Care: The spectrum of care services from independent living through to rest home, hospital, and dementia care, ideally provided on a single campus or through formal referral pathways.
